Su-27 Flanker

The Sukhoi Su‑27 Flanker is a Soviet/Russian twin‑engine air-superiority fighter developed by the Sukhoi Design Bureau. It began life in the late 1960s–early 1970s in direct response to the U.S. F‑15 Eagle. Similar to the F-16 during high-speed test flights, Soviet pilots encountered control-system quirks. One particularly test ended with a pilot pulling hard on pitch controls at high speed and unexpectedly forcing the Su‑27 into an early airborne recovery effectively turning an runway overrun into a take‑off maneuver. That unplanned lift‑off gave the Su‑27 its maiden flight on 20 May 1977. Although it entered service in the mid‑1980s, full-scale production ramped up only in the late Cold War.

Sknyliv Airshow Crash:

Tragically during an airshow in Ukraine on the 27th of July 2002 an Su-27 operated by the Ukrainian airforce crashed into a crowd of people killing 77 spectators and injuring over 500 others. This was due to pilot error and rushed flight planning that put the crowd in an unsafe place. This is the deadliest airshow ever and although so many died the pilots both safely ejected with no injuries. This shows fighter jets are powerful and safe but they can become extremely dangerous without precaution.

First Cobra Maneuver in Public:

During 1989 Paris Air Show an Su-27 and pilot Viktor Pugachev decided to show off to the audience with a cobra maneuver. The cobra maneuver is the most famous move where a pilot put a plane into a 90 degree turn without gaining height or losing it. It’s a difficult move that doesn’t have any advantage in a fight but looks cool in a airshow. This move has been seen and done in capable aircraft across the world since including Top Gun: Maverick and other shows.

Mig-21 Fishbed

The MiG‑21 “Fishbed” is a Soviet second‑generation supersonic fighter‑interceptor developed by the Mikoyan‑Gurevich Design Bureau. It emerged from a mid‑1950s Soviet requirement for a lightweight interceptor to replace the MiG‑17 and MiG‑19. The first flight occurred on February 14, 1955, putting it among the earliest Soviet aircraft to break Mach 2. It entered operational service in 1959, serving as the backbone of the Soviet Air Forces and being widely exported to over 50 countries the MiG‑21 became the most‑produced supersonic jet ever, with more than 11,000 built, and it remains in service in some countries even today.

The Mig 21’s were very successful due to the lack of a gun on most F4’s during the time and unreliability of low altitude launches of radar missiles allowing Mig 21’s to fly low and fast and then dogfight with their guns and IR (Infrared) missiles. Su-57 Felon

The Su‑57 Felon is a Russian fifth‑generation stealth multirole fighter, developed under the PAK FA programme by Sukhoi. The project began in the late 1990s as a successor to the MiG‑29 and Su‑27 and aimed to produce Russia’s first operational stealth fighter. During a pre‑delivery test of the first production Su‑57 in December 2019, a control‑system malfunction led to a crash 110 km from the Dzyomgi airfield. The pilot ejected safely, but the aircraft was destroyed just before it could be handed over to the Russian government. Although marketed as a competitor to the F-35, F-22, and J-20 it doesn’t actually compare.

MiG-29 Fulcrum

The MiG‑29 Fulcrum is a Soviet-era twin-engine multirole fighter built by Mikoyan to counter the U.S. It was like the F‑15 Eagle and F‑16 Fighting Falcon in that it was a lighter cheaper version of the Su-27. It emerged from the Soviet LPFI (Lightweight Frontline Fighter) program in the early 1970s as a nimble, cost‑effective complement to the larger Su‑27. MiG‑29 prototype first flew on October 6, 1977 and entered Soviet Air Force service in 1982. The MiG-29 has a very similar story to the F-16’s in being the light cheap version of the respective countries main fighter jet during the cold war.

This works through the Ukrainian version of the US Wild Weasels getting locked or tracked by a radar system and using the HARM’s to target the radiation beams that are tracking them. This allows the missile to know where the air defense and guide itself toward the last know location of these air defenses. The Ukrainian’s have used this to great effect by destroying a S-400 system which is supposedly one of the best long range air defense systems in the world and the best in Russia along with the S-500.
